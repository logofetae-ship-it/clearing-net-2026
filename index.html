How Clearing works — explained simply

Clearing is simply how you pick up a university place if, on results day, you don't end up holding your firm offer. You already have UCL at IB 38 — so you only ever touch any of this if you land below 38 and UCL doesn't take you. Here is the whole thing in plain steps, put together from your counsellor's UCAS session.

The dates that matter (Dubai time)

  • 6 July — your IB results reach you (~16:00 Dubai). Universities start processing straight away, but a decision can take a few days.
  • 2 July → 19 October — Clearing is open. The last day to add a Clearing choice is 19 October; final date for applications is 24 September.
  • 13 August — A-level (Level 3) results day. If you just miss UCL, they're allowed to wait until this date before making a final call, so a "not confirmed yet" on 6 July is not a rejection.

On results day — before you panic

Watch the status bar at the top of your UCAS Hub application. Three things can happen if you miss the 38:

  1. UCL still confirms you — universities often accept a near miss. Nothing else to do.
  2. UCL makes a "change of course offer" — same university, a different course/start date. You get 5 days to accept or decline, so read it carefully. This is a real route to still end up at UCL.
  3. Still deciding / waiting for documents — you may see a message asking for proof (e.g. a certificate). Send what they ask for; don't assume the worst.
  4. You fall into Clearing — even then, UCL itself has Clearing places (the UCL rows pinned at the top: Economics and Business, Management, and more). You'd apply on UCL's own Clearing form — a genuine way back into UCL, just on a different course.

You're actually in Clearing only if UCL and (if it applies) your Warwick insurance both fall through — and then a 6-digit Clearing number appears in your Hub next to your 10-digit Personal ID. Note UCL Clearing runs on UCL's own website, not UCAS, so apply there directly (line: +44 (0)20 8059 0939).

Have these ready before you call anyone: your UCAS Personal ID (the 10-digit number in your UCAS Hub) · your Clearing number (shows in the Hub once you're in Clearing) · your IB results. Say up front you're an overseas applicant, under 18 — ask about international fees, a CAS for the student visa, and their under-18 policy + guaranteed accommodation.

How to actually get a Clearing place — the steps

  1. Call the university directly (numbers below). Give your name, Personal ID and the course; ask if they'll take you with your results.
  2. Get a verbal "yes" on the phone — write down who you spoke to.
  3. Add it in the UCAS Hub as your Clearing choice — you can only hold one at a time, and you don't reply to a Clearing offer, you just add the choice and the university makes the decision. It shows "Waiting for confirmation", then "Confirmed" (by 21 October at the latest).
  4. If it comes back "Unsuccessful", you're free to add a different one. Keep going until one confirms.
  5. Then chase the CAS immediately for your student visa — for an overseas September start this is the tightest deadline, so start the day it's confirmed.

The firm / insurance rule (important for you)

You can't swap your firm and insurance. If UCL confirms, you're going to UCL and Warwick is automatically dropped. Your insurance (Warwick) is not in London — so if you miss UCL but make Warwick, you'd be confirmed at Warwick, and to take a London place instead you'd have to use "Decline my place" in the Hub to release yourself into Clearing. Never decline until a London university has already said yes on the phone — declining is one-way.
